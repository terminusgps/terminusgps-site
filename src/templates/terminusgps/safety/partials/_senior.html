Senior Security

Older adults are the fastest growing segment of our population, and with older drivers comes increased risk for driving related injury.

Risks on the Road

Currently, there are no standard reviews in place to determine when driving should be restricted or when it's time to give up the keys.

A driver's license signifies more than the ability to drive: it signifies freedom and self-sufficiency, a privilege not easily relinquished.

These decisions can be tough for the entire family.

Most senior drivers are not impaired by age alone, rather medical conditions and the use of prescription/over-the-counter medications.

According to AAA and NHTSA, having even one medical condition significantly increases driver risk and the likelihood of impaired driving.

According to AARP, getting lost and confusion are rated in the top five warning signs of unsafe driving for seniors.
