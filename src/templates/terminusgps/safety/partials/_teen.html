5 NOs to Drive

  1. No Cell Phones
  2. No Extra Passengers
  3. No Speeding
  4. No Alcohol
  5. No Driving/Riding without Seatbelts

Consequences of Speeding

We are all guilty of speeding, often to just keep with the flow of traffic.

However, it's bad practice, unsafe and unlawful.

Speeding is even more dangerous for teen drivers as they are more likely to lack the driving intuition experienced drivers take for granted on the road.

Teens may not be able to swiftly make critical decisions required under sudden changed weather conditions, road changes, road obstructions or gauging an oncoming vehicle while trying to pass.

Driver intuition only improves with experience and time.

Technology Does Help

We believe in positive reinforcement of good behavior, rather than emphasizing mistakes.

Remind your teen their safety is your foremost concern; establishing a level of trust is key.

Today's technology, like GPS monitoring and speed alerts, can be used in the reinforcement of good driving behaviors and to secure that level of trust between you and your teen driver.
