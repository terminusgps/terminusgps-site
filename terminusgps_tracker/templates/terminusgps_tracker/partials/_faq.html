How does it work?

The device is a cellular modem with a GPS receiver that transmits its geographic location as the vehicle moves around.

Typically, you will see a renewed location every 5 minutes when the vehicle is driving and every 12 hours if parked.

What is GPS?

The GPS is a "constellation" of approximately 30 well-spaced satellites that orbit Earth.

These satellites make it possible to pinpoint a specific geographic location using a ground receiver.

How reliable is the system?

We have a 99% reliability of the platform using multiple servers to keep you up-to-date with immediate location. At times the unit may lose cellular signal like your cell phone but the memory will hold the plotted information until it reconnects.

In the meantime, the device will immediately start looking for cell signal.

Sometimes when a vehicle is driven into a large parking structure that is underground or a metal building with few or no windows, the unit may lose GPS signal.

The unit will record its last good location and broadcast it with the message of lost signal.

When the vehicle emerges from the garage it will regain signal and continue as normal.
